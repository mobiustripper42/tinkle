CThe arming chain (fail-dry core)

ESP32 — the command key

Opens/closes the specific zone, sets the diverter, opens the master FET, enables the pump relay per schedule. Emits a heartbeat square wave on GPIO4 whenever firmware is healthy.

ATtiny85 — the arm key

Independent MCU. Trips if the heartbeat stops (~2s) or any run exceeds its own hard max-runtime ceiling (~30 min), measured on its own clock — it never trusts the ESP32's sense of time.

Safety relay — the gate

Normally-open, energize-to-pass. The ATtiny holds it closed only while armed. It feeds 24V to both the master and the pump. ATtiny trips → relay opens → master spring-closes, pump dies → no water.

DWiring notes that bite if skipped

Flyback on every coil

1N4007 (or Schottky) across the master solenoid, pump relay coil, and both relays. The DRV8871 has internal protection; the discrete FET and relays do not.

Flow sensor level shift

Power the sensor at 5V, but its pulse output swings to 5V — drop it to 3.3V (divider or the level-shifter module) before GPIO27, or you cook the input over time.

Input-only button pins

GPIO 34/35/39/36 have no internal pull-ups. Add 10k to 3.3V + a 100nF debounce cap on each button, even though firmware also debounces.

Diverter ≠ solenoid timing

The motorized ball valve travels ~5–6s and self-cuts at its limit switch. Drive its DRV8871 for the full travel window, not the 75ms latch pulse the Hunter solenoids use.

Common ground

Tie all buck grounds + 24V return + ESP32 GND together at one star point. Floating logic ground against a switching 24V rail is the classic intermittent-glitch source.

Master FET gate

IRLZ44N is logic-level and fine at 0.75A with a 3.3V gate. Add a 100Ω series gate resistor + 100k gate-to-GND pulldown so it sits off during ESP32 boot.

Zone 3 on strapping pins (DEC-007)

Z3's H-bridge sits on GPIO15 (MTDO) and GPIO12 (MTDI) because all non-strapping output GPIO are spent. This is safe only because the DRV8871's internal input pulldowns (~100k) hold both pins LOW through the boot strap window while the ESP32 pins are hi-Z: GPIO12 sampling low selects the correct 3.3V flash VDD, GPIO15 low is cosmetic (boot log), and both-low means no spurious valve pulse at boot. Never put an external pull-up — or a probe with one — on GPIO12: a high at boot selects 1.8V flash and bricks boot. Do not add Z3's own H-bridge pull resistors; rely on the DRV8871's internal ones.
